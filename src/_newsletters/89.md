<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Yes, the 1.5 billion-strong social network is going all-in on live video, just a year on from Twitter&#39;s official launch of its own service, Periscope.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Facebook isn&#39;t messing around with its live product &ndash; it&#39;s replacing the &#39;Messenger&#39; button in the main app with a constantly updating set of interesting streams and <a href="http://www.adweek.com/news/technology/facebook-will-pay-publishers-livestream-video-new-hub-170642" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">even paying publishers to do their own streams</a>.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Over the last few years Facebook has lamented how popular Snapchat became without it <a href="http://mashable.com/2014/01/06/snapchat-facebook-acquisition-2/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">after the service rejected multiple buyout offers over the years</a>. Live video tries to bring some of Snapchat&#39;s &#39;fun&#39; elements to video: live drawing, filters, silly overlays and more.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">BuzzFeed, naturally, immediately took it to the next level by broadcasting its team putting rubber bands on a watermelon to make it explode. It took 45 minutes and some 670 bands, but garnered 750,000 concurrent views &ndash; more than most broadcast shows.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s a big bet, but given that Facebook is struggling to get people to share original content directly, <a href="https://www.theinformation.com/facebook-struggles-to-stop-decline-in-original-sharing" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">with a 15 percent reduction in &#39;original&#39; sharing last year</a>, it&#39;s looking for any way to keep you active, in public. The company even created an entire division to get you to share more through small tweaks, like making your Newsfeed hint to share your photos.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">As mobile connections offer bigger amounts of data and faster connections thanks to LTE, Facebook might have the perfect storm for making &#39;live&#39; the new default.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">For developers, this is awesome news on many levels: mobile development could have an universal development language if this happens, removing the divide for switching to writing on any different platform.
