<p data-wr_replaced="true" style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">I was away for the majority of this week, so wasn&#39;t exactly on top of everything going on in the tech world, but iOS 9 was released on Wednesday with great fanfare, as always.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">A free update for iPhone 4s, 5, 5s, 6 and 6 Plus owners, as well as most iPad models, it&#39;s probably the first release that focuses on delivering serious stability and speed improvements, rather than swathes of new features. <a href="http://thenextweb.com/apple/2015/09/16/ios-9-review-apple-finally-focuses-on-stability-and-smarts/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">I wrote a review here, if you want to know what you&#39;re getting into</a>.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">There is some new stuff, like &#39;proactive Siri&#39; which predicts what you want to do sort of magically, improvements to core apps and more. If you haven&#39;t done the update already, it&#39;s seriously worth doing and doesn&#39;t appear to affect the speed of any of Apple&#39;s devices in much of a negative way.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Much of the discussion this week, however, circled around a single new feature in iOS 9 that Apple hasn&#39;t made much noise about: ad blocking. A new API allows developers to build apps that block advertising on mobile web for the first time and it&#39;s been quickly embraced, with a number of them <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/9/18/9351267/ad-blockers-top-iphone-chart-downloads" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">making it to the top 100 charts</a> on day one.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Publishers, naturally, are a little upset about the existence of them as they mark an extistential threat to their very business, so much has been written about their potential impact.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The biggest drama this week came when Marco Arment loudly launched his well-designed ad blocking app, Peace, which shot to the #1 spot worldwide. He <a href="http://www.marco.org/2015/09/18/just-doesnt-feel-good" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">suddenly pulled it two days later</a>, saying he felt &quot;wrong&quot; doing it and taking money away from other people. Plenty of other ad blockers will fill the void, but it shows just how much of an impact mobile ad blocking can have.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s a brave new world for many publishers who simply don&#39;t have an alternative. <a href="http://www.theawl.com/2015/09/welcome-to-the-block-party" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">The Awl published an amazing look at the state of blocking</a> and what it&#39;ll mean for all sizes of blogs, which will either disappear altogether or move toward more nefarious types of advertising, like native ads, which are much harder to block.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">To me, it feels like a storm in a teacup type situation. We&#39;re in the middle of a shakeout of those that can&#39;t adapt as crappy ads bottom out and platforms like Apple News, Facebook Instant Articles and others try to take control away altogether -- but ad blocking has existed for more than a decade and has only seen a relatively small amount of adoption.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">In the long term, things aren&#39;t looking very good at all, but I think the model will gradually shift to where the line blurs between &#39;content&#39; and &#39;ads&#39; to the point we can&#39;t tell anymore.</p>

<p class="muted" style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #ABABAB !important;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="http://thenextweb.com/apple/2015/09/16/ios-9-review-apple-finally-focuses-on-stability-and-smarts/" target="_blank" style="color: #ABABAB !important;text-decoration: none;">thenextweb.com</a></p>
