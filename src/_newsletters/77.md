<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Consumer Electronics Show was held in Las Vegas this week, which is the annual unveiling of futuristic gadgets that might never make it into the real world. Previous years have been somewhat lackluster, but this year highlighted just how close Virtual Reality is to hitting the mainstream, and how the Internet of Things might be invading our homes soon.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Oculus VR <a href="https://www.oculus.com/en-us/blog/oculus-rift-pre-orders-now-open-first-shipments-march-28/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">announced its preorders and pricing</a> -- starting at $599 and shipping as early as March -- and HTC <a href="http://www.roadtovr.com/hands-on-htc-vive-pre-brings-notable-improvements-to-visuals-tracking-and-ergonomics/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">demoed yet another iteration of the Vive</a> with improved tracking without giving real pricing or shipping dates.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The Oculus pricing triggered something of an outrage, <a href="http://www.slate.com/blogs/moneybox/2016/01/06/oculus_rift_s_price_sparks_backlash_but_gamers_are_buying_it_anyway.html" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">with many calling it overpriced for what it is</a>, but the reality is we&#39;re seeing the dawn of a new platform -- which Facebook is heavily subsidising -- and will drop in price over time.&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Otherwise, CES was nothing more than a swathe of &#39;smart&#39; gadgets from <a href="http://www.wired.com/2016/01/smart-fridges-samsung-lg/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">Samsung&#39;s ridiculous smart fridge</a>, to touchscreen-enabled ovens and <a href="http://au.ign.com/articles/2016/01/08/ces-2016-we-played-with-the-worlds-most-expensive-rubber-duck" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">Bluetooth rubber ducks</a>.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Gadget makers love the smart revolution, because it means devices likely have a shorter shelf life, but there are a number of questions about the future of IoT: will there be a common platform? Is security even a consideration for many of these products? Will my fridge <a href="http://motherboard.vice.com/read/smart-fridge-only-capable-of-displaying-buggy-future-of-the-internet-of-things" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">eventually stop getting software updates and break</a>?</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s undoubtable there&#39;s going to be a significant amount of &#39;smart&#39; devices that we all purchase in the future (I have a app-enabled thermostat already) but we need to be careful now about what we buy into.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">This week it emerged that Twitter is testing the water with going beyond 140 character tweets, all the way up to 10,000.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s not clear what they would look like exactly, but <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/digits/2016/01/05/twitter-to-expand-tweets-140-character-limit-to-10000/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">rumors pin the feature as more being an extension of tweets themselves</a>, sort of like a note attached that can be expanded when clicked on.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The change would be one of the most fundamental tweaks to how Twitter works, but is probably needed. The <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/mathonan/the-rise-of-the-screenshort" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">rise of screenshorts</a> has demonstrated how Twitter&#39;s constraints keep it from seeing deeper content on the service, but I&#39;m not sure that&#39;s the root of the problems.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">To me, Twitter&#39;s deepest problems are discoverability of friends or interesting people, rather than just slathering you with famous people to follow. But hey, they&#39;re going to keep tweaking this thing into the ground until something sticks.</p>

<h1 class="section-lead" style="color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;padding: 0;margin: 0;text-align: left;line-height: 1.3;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="http://fastcompany.com/3055208/its-true-apple-wil…" target="_blank" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Apple looks to drop headphone jack in iPhone 7</a></h1>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">In 2016, there&#39;s probably not going to be a bigger shitstorm than this: Apple plans to drop the 3.5mm headphone jack in favor of the Lightning port and Bluetooth later this year.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">According to reports, that&#39;s coming sooner than anyone expected as Apple looks for ways to both shrink the thickness of the iPhone and provide new ways for accessories, like noise-cancelling headphones, to interact with the iPhone.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s a change I&#39;m personally deeply upset about, but I can understand. It&#39;s sad that 3.5mm jacks, which are a universal standard, will go away since they&#39;re present everywhere from high-end audio systems to car line-ins, but the ubiquity of wireless accessories negates the need in the future.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">People are going to be <em>vehemently</em> upset about the change, but it&#39;s one I expect people will come to accept eventually. As headphones that can be powered over the Lightning port proliferate, and more goes wireless it makes sense that Apple, which always seems to take the painful route, will make the move first.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Still, let&#39;s take a moment to mourn the loss of the headphone jack, even if it hasn&#39;t happened just yet.</p>
