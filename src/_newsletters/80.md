<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The ride-sharing company this week unveiled a total overhaul of its branding from top to bottom, introducing a new logo, updated fonts, a new color palette and an &#39;atom&#39; identity.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">As far as redesigns go it&#39;s a pretty stark change from the recognizable big &#39;U&#39; on your homescreen. Instead, it&#39;s now a random circle with a bit out of the middle. Uber says the redesign is to reflect that it&#39;s more than just a ride-sharing company.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The update design isn&#39;t all that bad (in my opinion, at least) but it&#39;s not particularly well executed. It&#39;s just kind of confusing, and the way it was introduced was perhaps a little patronising to the average reader: &quot;We exist in the place where bits and atoms come together. That is Uber. We are not just technology but technology that moves cities and their citizens.&quot;</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="http://www.underconsideration.com/brandnew/archives/new_logo_and_identity_for_uber_done_in_house.php" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">Under Consideration has a scathing breakdown of the new design</a>, but what&#39;s particularly strange is the <a href="http://www.wired.com/2016/02/the-inside-story-behind-ubers-colorful-redesign/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">CEO&#39;s involvement in the design process</a>, who apparently was deeply involved in the year-long process to the point that he tried to learn basic design skills to best &#39;help&#39; the team.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">At the end of the day it&#39;s just a design, and all that matters is that you can still find Uber on your phone fairly easily. Perhaps the coolest part is in each country you open the app you&#39;ll see a different pattern and color as it loads&nbsp;to represent where you are -- regardless of all the faux outrage, it&#39;s still the app you&#39;ll probably use to get from A to B.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Is this the beginning of the end? (Probably not).</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Twitter reportedly plans to roll out an optional algorithmically generated timeline for the first time next week, which is likely to fundamentally change the way the service works.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s historically been the endless firehose of tweets in real time, but that can be overwhelming and tiring to keep up with. Assumably Twitter has noticed that people don&#39;t scroll down their timelines all that much, so it wants to provide a Facebook-style answer to seeing the most important tweets.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">If this is optional, it&#39;ll probably be a great change, given that Twitter has struggled to even maintain its current user base. <p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">There are a handful of adblocking browsers on Android, but until this week there hadn&#39;t been wide-spread support for adblocking in any major official browsers -- there&#39;s no motivation for Google to provide that, being an ad-funded company.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Samsung took that to the next level this week <a href="http://thenextweb.com/apps/2016/02/01/adblocking-comes-to-android-with-over-the-air-update-for-samsung-devices/#gref" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">by making available an API for adblocking extensions to take advantage of directly in the browser</a> it preloads on all its own devices.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It didn&#39;t last long, though, with Google beginning to pull adblockers from the store just 48 hours after they were published. It wouldn&#39;t say why they&#39;re removed, but it apparently had an issue with the fact that the adblocking took two apps to work.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">That&#39;s a strange excuse, given there are literally hundreds of addon apps on the Play Store that do exactly that -- more than anything it sounds like an excuse to stave off adblocking on the platform without ever explicitly actually saying it.</p>
