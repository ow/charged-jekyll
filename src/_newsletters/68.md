<td class="padded" style="word-break: break-word;-webkit-hyphens: auto;-moz-hyphens: auto;hyphens: auto;padding: 0px 0px 10px;vertical-align: top;text-align: left;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;margin: 0;line-height: 19px;font-size: 14px;border-collapse: collapse !important;padding-left: 15px !important;padding-right: 15px !important;padding-bottom: 0px !important;"><h1 class="section-lead" style="padding: 0px;margin-top: 0px;margin-right: 0px;margin-left: 0px;line-height: 1.3;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;margin: 0;text-align: left;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="http://thenextweb.com/twitter/2015/10/06/twitter-just-made-the-biggest-change-to-the-service-since-its-invention-moments/" target="_blank" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Twitter made its biggest change in history</a></h1>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">BIG news just 24 hours after Twitter announced it had&nbsp;<a href="http://www.wsj.com/articles/twitter-names-jack-dorsey-ceo-1444046580" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">selected Jack Dorsey to permanently run the company</a>: Project Lightning --&nbsp;<a href="http://thenextweb.com/twitter/2015/10/06/twitter-just-made-the-biggest-change-to-the-service-since-its-invention-moments/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">now called Moments</a>&nbsp;-- is here.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">If you&#39;re not familiar, Moments was a long-rumored feature that&#39;s promised to make Twitter more accessible for following along with live events like sports games, TV shows or news events.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The implementation is very interesting: a new tab for Moments shows at the bottom of the Twitter app on mobile. If you click into them, you get a rich timeline of the event, curated by the Twitter team to help tell the story.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Each Moment can feature tweets, pictures, videos, GIFs and other content -- in practice, they resemble Snapchat stories.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">For events that are unfolding a new &#39;follow&#39; button will appear that allows you to follow the event itself and have relevant tweets slide into your timeline automatically as it unfolds, then automatically unfollow it once it&#39;s over.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Moments is impressive on a lot of levels, as it makes Twitter far more accessible. First, it lets you follow things that interest you, perhaps the Emmys, temporarily, without needing to find specific people to follow. Second, it finally makes tweets have a longer life -- with Moments, they&#39;re able to be resurfaced later as part of a story.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Twitter won&#39;t let just anyone make a Moment, for good reason. It&#39;s assembling a curation team internally as well as a set of partners -- like BuzzFeed and Mashable -- to create custom Moments as well.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">On top of that, they&#39;re embeddable anywhere on the Web, giving the service wider appeal than ever before. Here&#39;s&nbsp;<a href="https://twitter.com/i/moments/651260177874853888" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">an example of a Moment from launch</a>.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">This is a&nbsp;<strong>big</strong>&nbsp;deal for Twitter, which is pinning its hopes and future on this feature -- for good reason. It&#39;s in desperate need of finding a way to reach a broader audience and this could be it. The preview I saw was very exciting, and <a href="https://stratechery.com/2015/twitters-moment/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">should encourage more people to try the service</a> -- it&#39;ll particularly help those who are signing up for the first time.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">I&#39;m looking forward to trying this -- at launch it&#39;s available for iOS, Android and Web users based in the US. It&#39;ll roll out elsewhere soon, apparently.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">An unfortunate side note with this - Twitter is <a href="https://recode.net/2015/10/09/twitter-is-planning-company-wide-layoffs-for-next-week/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">apparently planning</a> company-wide layoffs this coming week, to try and &#39;lean up&#39; the team.</p>

<p class="muted" style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #ABABAB !important;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="http://thenextweb.com/twitter/2015/10/06/twitter-just-made-the-biggest-change-to-the-service-since-its-invention-moments/" target="_blank" style="color: #ABABAB !important;text-decoration: none;">thenextweb.com</a></p>

<h1 class="section-lead" style="color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;padding: 0;margin: 0;text-align: left;line-height: 1.3;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="http://thenextweb.com/insider/2015/10/07/googles-plan-to-speed-up-the-mobile-web-burn-it-down/" target="_blank" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Google&#39;s plan to fix the (mobile) web</a></h1>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">An interesting move from Google today with the launch of Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP for short), a new technology that makes sites load in milliseconds instead of seconds.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s primarily targeted at publishers, which have big, slow websites and often end up losing visitors who give up waiting. The state of many sites today is that it can take between 5-10 seconds for the content to appear, which is far too long for most people&#39;s attention spans.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">AMP is an open source specification, so anyone can use it, and primarily gets speed gains from banning Javascript from running -- something news sites utilize heavily for advertising and user tracking. AMP allows advertising, but it has to be explicitly non-intrusive.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Most interestingly, Google is offering to host AMP content on behalf of anyone who opts in, so that it loads faster from its global network of servers.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">AMP is fascinating, because it comes to the market at a time when ad blockers have reached the limelight and companies like Facebook are fighting to get the news hosted on their own service. The problem is, it also highlights that news companies are often too incompetent or don&#39;t care enough to fix their pageload problems.</p>

<p style="margin-left: 0px;padding: 0px;margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">All that aside, this is an interesting opportunity. Google wants to help fix the web, and it&#39;s doing it in a way that doesn&#39;t lock companies in or force them to hand over a cut. It&#39;s optional to use AMP, it doesn&#39;t affect search ranking (yet) but it does deliver incredible speed.&nbsp;<a href="http://g.co/ampdemo" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">Check it out here</a>, if you&#39;re in the US/UK.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">In a landmark ruling, the European Union nullified its &#39;Safe Harbor&#39; agreement with the United States. The agreement allows technology companies to move European citizens&#39; data between countries despite traditionally strict controls on where it can be stored -- provided the companies can guarantee it&#39;s stored safely away from prying eyes.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The <a href="http://uk.businessinsider.com/european-court-of-justice-safe-harbor-ruling-2015-10" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">ruling this week was due to Snowden&#39;s earlier&nbsp;NSA spying revelations</a> and means that tech companies in particular may have a hard time in the future moving any European citizen&#39;s data between countries. They may be forced to quickly develop operations in Europe to comply with the law as the court case keeps moving forward.</p>
