<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The biggest, and probably most surprising, news in recent times came this week when Microsoft announced a partnership with Canonical to bring Ubuntu to Windows. What this means is developers can use the Bash shell on their desktop, without emulation, natively.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s an impressive feat of engineering &ndash; many web developers keep a Linux VM or use a Mac for this reason &ndash; which offers yet another big reason to&nbsp;a Windows PC as someone who writes code.&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Developers can now compile Linux code, use its toolchains and applications, without any hassle. Microsoft&#39;s researchers <a href="http://www.hanselman.com/blog/DevelopersCanRunBashShellAndUsermodeUbuntuLinuxBinariesOnWindows10.aspx" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">wrote a system that intercepts Linux system calls and translates them to Windows&#39; in real time</a>, so everything should work as you&#39;d expect.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Of course, the immediate thought that many had was that Microsoft may be back to old habits as it&#39;s losing in the Web-facing-server area. In the 90&#39;s it was well documented that the company used an &#39;embrace, extend, extinguish&#39; technique to win market dominance and it&#39;s possible this could be a similar play.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Regardless, the new Microsoft is incredibly open and it&#39;s refreshing to see it embracing developers&#39; needs so willingly. Apple, meanwhile, isn&#39;t making any noises on OS X at all, which I hope will change when WWDC rolls around in June.</p>
