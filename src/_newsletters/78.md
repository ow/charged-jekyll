<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Just days after announcing that Netflix was &#39;officially&#39; global and available in more than 130 countries, the company declared that it would be targeting users who circumvent geo-restrictions to get access to more Netflix content.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">On some regional versions of Netflix, like the Dutch one, the content you&#39;ll see is vastly less than what&#39;s available in the US. <p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="https://media.netflix.com/en/company-blog/evolving-proxy-detection-as-a-global-service" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">In a post on its blog</a>, Netflix acknowledged that it&#39;s working to make content more widely available, but &quot;for now, given the historic practice of licensing content by geographic territories, the TV shows and movies we offer differ, to varying degrees, by territory. In the meantime, we will continue to respect and enforce content licensing by geographic location.&quot;</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">There has been much hand wringing over what that means exactly, after Netflix announced last week it would crack down on these users and only show them what they&#39;re actually licensed to see, even if they&#39;re using a geo-unblock trick.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The company has historically turned a blind eye to these users, only minimally working to stop them, since they were paying customers anyway. <p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">According to the blog, you might start being locked out in a few weeks, though many are speculating that the company is just making a declaration to <em>appear</em> like it cares, to keep the rightsholders happy.</p>
