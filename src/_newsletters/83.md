<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">This week was the annual Mobile World Congress in Barcelona, where I spent five days hearing about the latest shiny devices phone companies (hint: not Apple) have been working on.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">There are a number of interesting new Android phones out there, like <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2016/2/21/11077616/lg-g5-announced-specs-release-date-price-mwc-2016" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">LG&#39;s unique G5</a>, which allows you to hot-swap the battery for expanded accessories, like a camera grip or high-end DAC for audiophiles. Cool feature, but it&#39;s unclear if LG is committed to modularity long term, so you might buy the modules this time and find they don&#39;t work with the G6.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="http://www.theverge.com/2016/2/21/11077956/samsung-galaxy-s7-edge-smartphone-announced-specs-mwc-2016" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">Samsung&#39;s new Galaxy S7 also came out of the box</a> and while it looks unsurprisingly similar to last year&#39;s version it&#39;s actually a surprisingly compelling option, given that the company has embraded the ecosystem and <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2016/2/21/11081450/samsung-gear-360-camera-video-specs-mwc-2016" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">built a VR 360-degree camera</a> (no price or availability yet) and a VR headset ($99) that can view that footage.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Out of all the phone makers, Xiaomi blew me out of the water. Its <a href="http://thenextweb.com/gadgets/2016/02/24/xiaomis-mi-5-is-a-stunning-5-inch-android-phone/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">new Mi 5 smartphone is a powerful beast</a> with Snapdragon 820 processor (the latest), 4 GB of RAM, a great camera. and impressive design... for about $300. The catch? You probably can&#39;t actually buy it outside of India and China.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">If I noticed anything this week, it&#39;s that everyone <em>except</em> Apple is publicly betting on virtual reality as a way to keep customer interest -- and while it seems like a gimmick, people love it. Devices like the <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2016/2/21/11081450/samsung-gear-360-camera-video-specs-mwc-2016" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">Gear 360</a> really help people understand why VR is so cool, especially when you can film your own world for reliving later.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Microsoft reached a deal this week to acquire Xamarin, a platform that makes it easier for developers to use Microsoft&#39;s products to build iOS and Android apps without needing to learn the idiosyncracies of each platform.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">As Windows Phone has basically died, Microsoft&#39;s strategy has shifted to purchasing other popular productivity apps for iOS and Android as a way to stake claim, since it won&#39;t have its own platform.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">With the purchase of Xamarin, it&#39;s now paving the way to make it easy for developers to ship apps that work across iOS, Android and Windows.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">If you could write code once for the Universal Windows Platform and have it run on every platform -- even non-Microsoft ones, that&#39;s a compelling reason to live in the company&#39;s world.</p>
