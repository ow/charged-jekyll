<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Mailbox, the popular email app for many of us, hadn&#39;t seen any updates for iOS, Mac and Android in months which lead many to suspect Dropbox had stopped development internally without saying anything.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">That became reality on Monday when Dropbox announced that it was killing both Mailbox and Carousel, its image uploading app. In early 2016, the apps will stop functioning entirely because of what Dropbox labels as &quot;refocusing&quot; on its core.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Ultimately what appears to be going on at Dropbox is a shakeout. <p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Now that Mailbox is dead, you&#39;re probably looking for a new email app. There are still a bunch of great choices, and here are the ones I&#39;m a fan of:</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/5/28/8673523/best-iphone-email-app-outlook-gmail-apple-mail-ios" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">Outlook by Microsoft</a>: a great email client that rolls calendar and contacts into a single app, so you don&#39;t need to have multiple. Works very similar to Mailbox and available on <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/microsoft-outlook/id951937596?mt=8" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">iOS</a> and <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.microsoft.office.outlook&hl=en" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">Android</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://readdle.com/products/spark" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">Spark Mail</a>: A new contender, but a good one. Intelligently separates your important email from the noise, and it&#39;s free! Only on iOS but coming to Mac soon.</li>
<li><a href="http://airmailapp.com/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">Airmail</a>: A simple and attractive email app for Mac and iOS that works great with Gmail.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.postbox-inc.com/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">Postbox</a>: Honestly, the Windows email client market is pretty bleak. Postbox is a great option, however, that works on both Windows and Mac. It&#39;s not super pretty, but it gets email done.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.nylas.com/n1" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">N1</a>: Attractive, feature-rich email app for Mac that works great with Gmail, iCloud and Microsoft accounts. It&#39;s open-source, so developers can easily extend it.</li>
<li><a href="https://polymail.io/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026;text-decoration: none;">Polymail</a>: An upcoming invite-only option for Mac that looks great.</li>
</ul>
