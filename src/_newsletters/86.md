<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Twitter <a href="http://thenextweb.com/twitter/2016/03/17/twitter-quietly-turned-new-algorithmic-timeline-everyone/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">started rolling out its new algorithm to everyone this week</a>, which means you&#39;ll start seeing out of order tweets at the top of your timeline soon. <p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Instagram is doing the same soon, placing the photos and videos it thinks you&#39;ll want to see the most regardless of when they&#39;re shared at the top of your feed. <p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">According to Instagram&#39;s co-founders, people miss more than 70 percent of their friends&#39; posts, so it&#39;s easy to see why it&#39;s looking to make the change: to make you stick around.</p>
