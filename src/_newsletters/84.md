<td class="padded" style="word-break: break-word;-webkit-hyphens: auto;-moz-hyphens: auto;hyphens: auto;padding: 0px 0px 10px;vertical-align: top;text-align: left;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;margin: 0;line-height: 19px;font-size: 14px;border-collapse: collapse !important;padding-left: 15px !important;padding-right: 15px !important;padding-bottom: 0px !important;"><h1 class="section-lead" style="color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;padding: 0;margin: 0;text-align: left;line-height: 1.3;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="http://thenextweb.com/apps/2016/03/02/slacks-friction-free-conference-calling-for-your-entire-team-is-awesome/#gref" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Slack takes on calling</a></h1>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">On Monday Slack started quietly rolling out a preview version of its calling feature, which allows you to place one-click audio calls one-to-one or inside a room full of people.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">On its own, this wouldn&#39;t be that exciting, but Slack&#39;s implementation is one of the best I&#39;ve seen. When you push the call button within a channel, it lets everyone know a call is ongoing and if you click, you&#39;re thrown into the call within a few seconds.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">No plugins, external apps or weirdness... just a call. Everyone&#39;s messed around with Skype trying to get it working properly, so understands the pain of conference calling tools.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s using <a href="https://webrtc.org/" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">WebRTC</a> underneath to deliver the calling capability, which is a relatively new, but interesting technology built into the HTML standard to allow plugin-free audio and video calls -- in the future it&#39;s likely Slack will expand into video too.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Slack might have just built something that will fix it once and for all by removing all the friction from the equation. It&#39;s still rolling out, but you should get it soon.</p>

<p class="muted" style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #ABABAB !important;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="http://thenextweb.com/apps/2016/03/02/slacks-friction-free-conference-calling-for-your-entire-team-is-awesome/" target="_blank" style="color: #ABABAB !important;text-decoration: none;"><strong>thenextweb.com</strong></a> or <a href="http://podcast.char.gd/episode-001/" target="_blank" style="color: #ABABAB !important;text-decoration: none;"><strong>listen on Charged Podcast</strong></a></p>

<h1 class="section-lead" style="color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;padding: 0;margin: 0;text-align: left;line-height: 1.3;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="http://techcrunch.com/2016/03/03/amazon-adds-the-130-tap-and-the-90-dot-to-the-echo-family/" target="_blank" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Amazon Echo just got way more interesting</a></h1>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">The cylindrical Amazon Echo speaker was always curious to me, though something of a gimmick, since it was tied to a single room and required using its own crappy speakers.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Amazon&#39;s made Echo far more interesting this week with the release of Dot and Tap, two new speakers. Dot plugs into any stereo system to give it Echo&#39;s smarts, so you could wire it up to an existing pair of speakers but still get the ability to use your voice to set timers or skip a song.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">That gives infinite more value to Echo because <em>any</em> speakers can be made smart for a relatively low price. The Tap, on the other hand, is a tiny portable Bluetooth speaker you can take anywhere, and offers the same.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">That one&#39;s a bit more weird -- Amazon sells it as a great sounding speaker to take anywhere that just happens to contain Echo, but I can&#39;t see myself saying &#39;Alexa, remind me to get toilet paper&#39; when I&#39;m at the beach or park.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Still, Echo is becoming a formidable player in the fight for the smart home. You can use Echo and your voice to change your thermostat or turn devices on or off now, which is far more compelling than messing around with Siri to get it working.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">For weeks the FBI has been pushing to force Apple&#39;s hand and make it unlock the iPhone at the <a href="http://uk.businessinsider.com/apple-shares-argument-for-not-hacking-san-bernardino-iphone-2016-2?op=1?r=US&amp;IR=T" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">center of the San Bernardino case</a>, but the company has vehemently refused.
