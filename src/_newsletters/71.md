<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">It&#39;s been rumored for years, but the Wall Street Journal reported this week that Google is actively working on folding Chrome OS into Android and pushing that on laptops instead, beginning in 2017.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">According to WSJ sources, Google will rename Chromebooks and drop the Chrome name from desktop/mobile browsers alongside the announcement. The change is supposed to be a way to get more developers interested in Google&#39;s desktop OS, which makes a lot of sense if you look at the state of Chrome OS today. It&#39;s quite empty.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Android as it stands is pretty awful on computers, but third-party projects like <a href="http://www.jide.com/en/remixos" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">RemixOS</a> show how awesome it could be, if Google made it an area of focus. I expect this move will happen eventually, but the transition might be painful for those with Chrome devices.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Google gave us a glimpse into this future earlier this year when it <a href="http://www.theverge.com/2015/9/29/9417313/pixel-c-hands-on-android-tablet-photos-video" target="_blank" style="color: #D12026 !important;text-decoration: none;">unveiled the Pixel C</a>, a laptop that runs Android, but it couldn&#39;t do much more than surf the web.</p>

<p class="muted" style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #ABABAB !important;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="http://www.wsj.com/article_email/alphabets-google-to-fold-chrome-operating-system-into-android-1446151134-lMyQjAxMTA1NzIxOTAyMzk4Wj" target="_blank" style="color: #ABABAB !important;text-decoration: none;">wsj.com</a></p>

<hr style="color: #DBDBDB;background-color: #d9d9d9;height: 1px;border: none;margin-top: 15px;margin-bottom: 15px;">
<h1 class="section-lead" style="color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;padding: 0;margin: 0;text-align: left;line-height: 1.3;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="https://newsroom.fb.com/news/2015/10/a-more-useful-notifications-tab-on-mobile/" target="_blank" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Facebook reinvented the notifications tab</a></h1>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">As people spend more and more time on Facebook the company has been looking for more ways to keep you there.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">A new notifications tab rolling out to the world brings a Google Now style view, with info like TV shows screening today, the weather in your area and friends&#39; life events you might have missed.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">This actually looks great, and is a big step from Facebook to give you fewer reasons to Google something or go to Siri. The feature learns from your location history and recommends interesting events in your area as well as things it thinks you might like to be aware of.</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">Looking forward to trying this -- out of all the companies building &#39;smart&#39; notifications like this, Facebook is the best positioned to deliver reliable, valuable recommendations.</p>

<p class="muted" style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #ABABAB !important;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;"><a href="https://newsroom.fb.com/news/2015/10/a-more-useful-notifications-tab-on-mobile/" target="_blank" style="color: #ABABAB !important;text-decoration: none;">facebook.com</a></p>

<h1 class="section-lead" style="color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: 700;padding: 0;margin: 0;text-align: left;line-height: 1.3;word-break: normal;font-size: 20px;margin-bottom: 10px;"><a href="https://www.fastcompany.com/3052809/app-economy/microsoft-outlook-mobile-apps-get-more-polish-and-just-a-dash-of-sunrise" target="_blank" style="color: #222222 !important;text-decoration: none;">Microsoft merging Sunrise with Outlook, killing standalone app</a></h1>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">About a year after Microsoft acquired both companies, it&#39;s announced that Sunrise will be merged with its Outlook mobile app (formerly Accompli) and that Sunrise will eventually go away once both apps reach &#39;feature parity.&#39;</p>

<p style="margin: 0;line-height: 25px;margin-bottom: 10px;color: #222222;font-family: &quot;Lato&quot;, &quot;Helvetica&quot;, &quot;Arial&quot;, sans-serif;font-weight: normal;padding: 0;text-align: left;font-size: 14px;">For me, this move is disappointing.
